How to Properly Stock Your Vacation Rental: A Guide to Happy Guests and Great Reviews

Properly stocking your vacation rental can make the difference between a one-time stay and a five-star review. Modern travelers expect comfort, convenience, and thoughtful details that turn a rental into a welcoming home away from home.

1. Essentials First: Cover the Basics

Kitchen

  • Dinnerware — plates, bowls, glasses, mugs, and utensils for max occupancy plus extras
  • Cookware — pots, pans, baking sheets, cutting boards, cooking utensils
  • Tools — can opener, wine opener, scissors
  • Appliances — microwave, toaster, coffee maker with filters
  • Pantry staples — salt, pepper, and basic spices
  • Cleaning — dish soap, sponges, paper towels, trash bags

Bathroom

  • Towels — bath, hand, and washcloths, minimum two sets per guest
  • Toiletries — hand soap, toilet paper, tissues
  • Hair dryer, plunger, and toilet brush

Bedroom

  • Fresh linens, extra pillows, and blankets
  • Hangers, drawer access, and an alarm clock or charging station

Cleaning Supplies

  • Broom, dustpan, mop, and vacuum (essential for pet-friendly units)
  • All-purpose cleaner and disinfectant wipes

2. Go Beyond the Basics: Add Comfort and Convenience

Comfort Upgrades

  • Blackout curtains or eye masks
  • Fans or space heaters
  • Quality mattresses with varied firmness options
  • Welcome mats and shoe racks

Tech & Entertainment

  • Reliable Wi-Fi with clear access instructions
  • Smart TV with streaming subscriptions (Netflix, Disney+)
  • Books, board games, playing cards, and a Bluetooth speaker

3. Local Touches Make a Big Impact

Guests love a welcome binder that includes house rules, emergency contacts, restaurant recommendations, grocery store and pharmacy locations, nearby attractions, public transit details, parking guidance, and local maps. Consider adding locally-sourced snacks, coffee, or beverages to make the welcome feel truly personal.

4. Stock for Stays of All Lengths

Long-term guests need more than a weekend visitor. Make sure longer stays are covered with:

  • Extended toiletries and extra toilet paper
  • Laundry essentials — detergent, fabric softener, stain treatment
  • Iron and ironing board
  • Reusable shopping bags and additional cookware and storage containers

5. Think Seasonally

  • Summer: Sunscreen, beach towels, outdoor games, fans
  • Winter: Throw blankets, hot chocolate, snow shovels, sleds
  • Spring/Fall: Allergy-friendly items (air purifiers), umbrellas

6. Safety & Accessibility

  • Smoke and carbon monoxide detectors
  • Fire extinguisher and first aid kit
  • Flashlights with batteries and hallway night lights
  • Clearly labeled light switches and remotes
  • Non-slip bathroom mats

Final Tip: Replenish and Refresh Regularly

After each guest checkout, run a complete inventory review and restock supplies. Keep a checklist to make restocking faster, and actively ask guests for feedback on what they appreciated — or wished was there. Thoughtful stocking creates an experience, not just a space, encouraging repeat visits and word-of-mouth recommendations.
